Abstract:

Recently, sequence-to-sequence-based models have been successfully applied in text-to-speech (TTS) to synthesize speech for single-language text. To synthesize speech for code-switch text usually requires multi-lingual speech from the target speaker. However, it is hard to collect these data. In this paper, we investigate transfer learning for code-switch TTS, where we utilize code-switch speech from Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) in TTS to synthesize code-switch speech for our target speaker. As a result, our proposed methods enable to generate better code-switch speech for the target speaker in terms of naturalness and speaker consistency. To further investigate the effect of transcription of the code-switch speech, we propose two ways of transfer learning. We found that even without transcription, semi-supervised pre-training also helps improving the model performance, which opens the door of getting full usage of publicly-found code-switch speech.

Note:

Here, we presents the synthesized code-switch speech by all the model variants in the paper. All of the utterances are unseen in training and development. We use Griffin-Lim to convert the predicted spectrogram into wavform for fast experiment cycles. We group the code-switch text into two groups: (1) those begins with Mandarin (2) those begins with English. We provide the speech demos for two groups.

System investigated in the paper:

        Tac: the model trained with only mono-lingual speech data from two female speakers;

        Pre1: the model which is pre-trained with only code-switch speech in a semi-supervised mode, then fine-tuned with mono-lingual speech data from two female speakers;

        Pre2: the model which is pre-trained with code-switch text-speech pairs, then fine-tuned with mono-lingual speech data from two female speakers;

        Mix: the model trained with mixture of code-switch text-speech pairs and mono-lingual speech data from two female speakers
